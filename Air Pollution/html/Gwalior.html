Gwalior

The Central Pollution Control Board's data puts Gwalior at the very top of India's eleven most polluted cities in terms of particulate matter. According to the pollution control board (CPCB), the particulate matter in Gwalior stands at 329 micrograms per cubic metre, which is nearly five times the permissible limit of 70 micrograms. The spurt in particulate matter pollution may have been caused due to factors such as high population density, increase in the number of vehicles, less greenery, and dilapidated roads. Gwalior's proximity to Delhi and other cities may have also played a role in some way.The rise in population is due to the large number of small industrial units that have sprouted up within city limits. Such industrial plants add pollutants to the air, and need to be moved out of Gwalior. Vehicular pollution contributed the most to the particulate matter in the air. It is not just the emissions, rather when vehicles ply on dilapidated roads in hot conditions, they kick up a lot of dust and particulate matter. The reduction in green cover both inside and around Gwalior isn't helping either.
